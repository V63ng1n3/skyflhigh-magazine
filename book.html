01 / Boxing Fundamentals

Boxing Fundamentals

Your core balance and alignment blueprint
  • Protect your chin with your fist at all times while using your elbow to lock your stomach sides
  • Give off your shoulder (a bit not too much), hip and leg instead of your chest squared up with your face straight.
  • A line connecting your toe (lead foot) to the heel of your back(rear) leg should remain to maintain balance. Maintain a certain fighting distance between both legs where you feel most balanced.
  • One leg flat footed, One leg on toe. On a regular stance, to get balance; lead foot flat footed and slightly slanted 60°-70° to the 180° line, rear foot's toe should be at angle 180° while on toe (heels shouldn't touch the ground).
  • While moving, whatever direction you are going land on toe first.

02 / Boxing Movement

Boxing Movement

The Box-Step coordination system

Movement Catalog:

  1. Step In, Step Out
  2. Step forward
  3. Pull back, return
  4. Step backward
  5. Half side step left, return
  6. Full Side step left
  7. Half side step right, return
  8. Full Side step right

Step In and Step Out

This is where the lead foot goes in or forward while rear foot stays in position. The step out is returning the lead foot back to its original position.

Step forward

Simply after stepping in, instead of returning your lead leg back to original position, you take your rear leg which is on toe forward. And you have moved forward this way. Adjust/Reset yourself to find balance by following the fundamentals.

The Pull back and step backward apply same principles likewise the half steps and full steps.

03 / Defensive Drills

Defensive Drill

Drill and High Guard Integration

Focus on the Boxing movement till you achieve a flow state where it becomes part of you. Remember to always hold your chin tight while moving but keep your mind in a relaxed state.

Beginner Drill

While performing the box step, whenever you step in or step forward, always move with the high guard.

High Guard: The High guard is essential for blocking straight punches like the Jab (1) and Cross (2). While holding your chin and your elbow protects your body, take your fists from the position of your chin to your head while ensuring your vision is not disturbed (try not to blink or use your gloves or hands to cover your eyes).

Apply the high guard whenever you move forward since it's your opponent's range you are entering and you can get punched.
